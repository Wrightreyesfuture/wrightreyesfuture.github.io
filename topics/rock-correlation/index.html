Rock Correlation Methods

Learn how geologists correlate rock layers across different locations to understand Earth's history.

Lithologic Correlation

Matching rock layers based on their physical characteristics such as color, texture, and mineral composition.

Fossil Correlation

Using index fossils to match rock layers across different locations.

Key Bed Correlation

Using distinctive rock layers that formed over a wide area in a short time period as marker horizons.

Index Fossils

Click to reveal definition

Fossils that are widely distributed geographically but existed for a relatively short time period, making them useful for dating and correlating rock layers.

Key Bed

Click to reveal definition

A distinctive rock layer that formed over a wide area in a short time period, useful as a marker horizon for correlation.

Stratigraphic Column

A diagram showing the vertical arrangement of rock layers in a particular area, with the oldest rocks at the bottom and youngest at the top.
