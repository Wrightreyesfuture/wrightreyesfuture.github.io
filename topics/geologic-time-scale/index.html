The Geologic Time Scale

Learn about the major divisions of Earth's 4.6 billion year history and the significant events that occurred during each time period.

Practice Activities

Apply your knowledge of the geologic time scale with these practice activities.

Eon

The largest division of geologic time, consisting of two or more eras.

Era

A division of geologic time that is smaller than an eon but larger than a period.

Period

A division of geologic time that is smaller than an era but larger than an epoch.

Review Flashcards

Precambrian

Click to reveal information

The earliest part of Earth's history, from the formation of the planet to the beginning of the Cambrian Period (4.6 billion to 541 million years ago).

Paleozoic Era

Click to reveal information

The era from 541 to 252 million years ago, known for the development of fish, amphibians, reptiles, and the first forests.

Mesozoic Era

Click to reveal information

The era from 252 to 66 million years ago, known as the "Age of Dinosaurs" and saw the first birds and mammals.

Cenozoic Era

Click to reveal information

The current era, from 66 million years ago to the present, known as the "Age of Mammals" and includes the evolution of humans.
